Coronavirus LIVE Updates: India's Death Toll Rises to 1,223 as Positive Cases Near 38,000-mark

Coronavirus LIVE Updates: The central government today said the death toll due to the novel coronavirus in India has risen to 1,223 and the number of positive cases has increased to 37,776. This number includes 26,535 active cases and 10,018 patients who have been cured/discharged/migrated. The Tamil Nadu government today said it will not allow any relaxations in containment zones across the state during the third phase of the lockdown from May 4. Standalone shops in non-containment zones, except hair salons and beauty parlours, can function from 11 am to 5 pm, while shops selling essential commodities will function between 6 am to 5 pm. Restaurants can function from 6 am to 9 pm but only with takeaway services.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Saturday held a series of meetings with key ministers, including Home Minister Amit Shah and Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman, and officials of economic ministries to firm up the second stimulus package for sectors impacted by lockdown to curb spread of coronavirus, sources said. The prime minister held discussions with Shah and Sitharaman and would have follow up meetings with ministers of key economic ministries such as Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME), sources said. The finance ministry, which deferred release of monthly GST collection numbers on Friday, is also scheduled to make a detailed presentation to the prime minister later in the day on the state of economy and several initiatives that it plans to undertake to stimulate Indian economy. As many as 41 people from one building in Theke Wali Gali near the District Commissioner's office in Kapashera, south west Delhi, have tested psoitive for coronavirus. The building was sealed on April 19 after one person from the building was diagnosed with the virus.
